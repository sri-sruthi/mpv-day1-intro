// about me

A bit different from most data scientists.

I came to data science not because I love numbers, but because I love understanding systems — the kind that affect people. I ask "is this actually useful?" before I ask "does this model converge?"

Psychology fascinates me. How people think, why they make the decisions they do, what makes something feel trustworthy — these questions shape how I approach every problem I work on.

People often find me easy to talk to. I think that's because I listen to understand, not just to respond. I care about what you're building and why you're building it.

🧠 Human behaviour curious 🤝 Team-first mindset 🔍 Pattern thinker 🌊 Calm under pressure 🌱 Perpetual learner

// the rest of me

Not on the resume.

The things that actually shape how I think and work.

🧠

Psychology & Human Behaviour

This is one of the biggest things shaping how I think. I'm fascinated by why people decide what they decide, how trust is built and broken, and why some systems feel intuitive while others don't. Learning about cognitive biases, behavioural economics, and social psychology has genuinely made me a better engineer. When you build something that touches people, you need to understand people first.

🌀

Meaning over speed

I've always been more drawn to depth than breadth. I'd rather spend time on one thing that genuinely matters than scatter attention across many things that don't. This sometimes makes me the "quiet one" — but I show up fully when I show up.

🤝

Approachable by nature

People often feel comfortable talking to me. I listen without rushing, try to understand before responding, and naturally lean toward encouragement over judgement. That mindset shapes how I collaborate as much as how I communicate.

🔭

Currently fascinated by

AI alignment and the ethics of what we build. How language shapes cognition. Why some teams are extraordinary and others aren't. What makes a piece of communication truly land. The gap between knowing and understanding.

⚡

What energises me

Building something from scratch and seeing it actually used. A conversation where both people leave thinking differently. Elegant solutions that make you ask "why didn't everyone do it this way?" Finding the real question behind the question asked.

🎯

Why I'm here

Marketing and AI are converging fast. The brands that understand behaviour — psychologically and technically — will win. I want to be at that intersection. And I want to make things that feel genuinely good, not just clever.
